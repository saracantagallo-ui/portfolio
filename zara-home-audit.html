Product detail page (PDP)

The PDP preserves visual consistency across devices, but the purchase journey lacks a clear behavioral hierarchy. Some friction points affect both desktop and mobile, while others are device-specific.

  • Unclear primary action Desktop Mobile Add-to-cart does not visually dominate enough to clearly signal the next step.
  • Accordion discoverability Mobile Expandable sections lack strong visual affordance, reducing interaction confidence.
  • Decision hierarchy confusion Desktop Quantity selector sits too close to size/options, increasing the risk of perceived overlap.
  • Recommendation overload Desktop Large related-product grids dilute focus from the core purchasing decision.
Design goal Separate decision layers (variants vs quantity), reinforce the primary action, and structure content progressively depending on device context.
02 Solution

Redesign strategy

Hierarchy + Progressive disclosure

Preserve the editorial identity, but introduce a clear orientation system: stronger visual hierarchy, consistent patterns across desktop/mobile, explicit interaction cues, and reduced cognitive load.

Navigation architecture

A clear multi-level structure that separates products, collections, editorial, and services, improving information scent and predictability.

Homepage: more modular, less intrusive

A calmer hero, clearer category entry points, and modules that communicate priority and action. The experience remains editorial ‚Äî but becomes navigable.

  • Modular structure: clear sections (special selection, new in, collaborations).

PDP: a clearer funnel

Separate quantity vs variants, use explicit tabs (desktop) and accordions (mobile), and reduce recommendation overload. The product stays the hero, and the action becomes obvious.
