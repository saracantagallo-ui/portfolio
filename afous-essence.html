Case Study: Afous Essence

Crafting a
Heritage Bridge.

A strategic ecosystem designed to elevate Moroccan craftsmanship. Balancing authentic narrative with a robust B2B/B2C architecture to eliminate friction in international sourcing.

Built from zero: the project started without an existing digital product or brand system. My role was to define the full UX strategy: information architecture, positioning, user paths, and visual direction so the experience could scale beyond aesthetics.

MY ROLE

UX Strategist & Lead Designer

CONTEXT

E-commerce Artisan Luxury

STATUS

Low-Fi Prototyping

The Challenge

Moroccan craftsmanship is tactile, slow, and human. Most digital platforms fail to translate this "soul" into a screen, resulting in a loss of perceived value. The goal was to design a dual-path platform (B2B/B2C) that protects heritage while enabling modern conversion, without compromising trust, authenticity, and operational clarity.

02 Strategy & Solution

Experience Strategy: Designing the Ideal Journey

Before defining pages, navigation, or interactions, the project required a clear experiential direction. The goal was to translate Afous Essence’s brand values into a coherent, trust-driven journey — balancing emotional discovery with professional confidence.

Future-State Journey Map. Experience strategy defining phases, emotions, and touchpoints for the post-launch vision.

The Strategic Framework

The experience strategy defines how Afous Essence should feel at every stage of the journey. Rather than focusing on screens or clicks, this phase establishes emotional goals, trust-building moments, and the role of each touchpoint in shaping perception.

  • Discovery-driven experience: users are guided through culture, craftsmanship, and storytelling before product comparison.
  • Trust as a design goal: provenance, maker visibility, and transparent processes reduce friction and uncertainty.
  • Dual decision logic: emotional engagement for consumers and operational clarity for professional buyers.

Strategic Outcome

A clear experiential north star that informed all subsequent design decisions: from information architecture to interaction patterns.

03 Branding

Logo Meaning, Palette & Moodboard Direction

Branding was built to translate Moroccan identity into a modern premium system, without clichés. The word Afous (hand) becomes a symbol of craft, care, and human-made value: “the hand of Morocco in every detail”.

Typography and UI tone are designed to feel curated and editorial, with Gloock used for headlines to convey heritage and authority, and Karla for body text to ensure clarity, readability, and frictionless conversion flows.

Typography Hierarchy

  • Headlines Gloock · 36–48 pt
  • Subheadings Gloock · 24 pt
  • Body text Karla · 11–13 pt
  • Captions & microcopy Karla · 10 pt

The typographic hierarchy was designed to balance expressiveness and clarity. This contrast supports storytelling without compromising usability or conversion.

Brand Direction

Visual exploration focused on translating Moroccan heritage into a contemporary and scalable brand system.

Moodboard Materials, textures, palette, atmosphere

The visual language was designed to feel tactile and authentic, while supporting trust and clarity across both B2C and B2B experiences.

Logo Meaning

The Afous Essence logo represents the connection between the artisan’s hand and the essence of contemporary design. The abstract symbol is inspired by the Berber hand, “Afous”, reinterpreted through a minimal and geometric language.

  • Pictogram: human gesture, tradition, culture.
  • Afous: “hand” in Berber — craft and identity.
  • Essence: design purity, refinement, intention.
